Understanding CNN Architecture for Fruit Classification

Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) have revolutionized the field of image classification. In our Smart Sorting project, we use a custom CNN architecture to classify fruits into six categories: Fresh Apple, Fresh Banana, Fresh Orange, Rotten Apple, Rotten Banana, and Rotten Orange.

The model consists of two convolutional layers with max-pooling, followed by a series of fully connected dense layers. The architecture uses the ReLU activation function for hidden layers and Softmax for the output layer, enabling multi-class classification.

Data augmentation techniques including shearing, zooming, and horizontal flipping are applied to the training images, which helps the model generalize better and avoid overfitting on the limited training dataset of 1,212 images.
